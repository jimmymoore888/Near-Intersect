NEAR INTERSECT VOLCANO — ERUPTION PHASE MODEL (LOCKED) SYSTEM RULE (PUBLIC + LEGAL – SIMPLE VERSION) Eruptions happen automatically when the system reaches a pressure milestone. No person, owner, or system can manually trigger an eruption. Think of it like this: When the volcano fills up to a line, it erupts on its own. When an eruption happens: • A portion is shared equally (distributed) • A portion is kept (reserved) for future growth As the system grows, it follows a stable structure: • 60% is shared equally • 40% is reserved until the next milestone is reached Larger milestones only occur if real people actively use the system. Nothing is forced.
