Eruption Law

Eruptions happen automatically when the system reaches a pressure milestone. No person, owner, or system can manually trigger an eruption.

Think of it like this: when the volcano fills up to a line, it erupts on its own.

When an eruption happens, a portion is shared equally and a portion is reserved for future growth.

Phase 1 is a Genesis Bootstrap Exception: 75% distributed / 25% retained.

After Phase 1, the system follows the standard structure: 60% shared equally / 40% reserved until the next milestone is reached.

Larger milestones only occur if real participation supports scale. Nothing is forced. Nothing is guaranteed.
